Bréf íslensku þingmannanna til Þingmannasambands Nató

Í tilefni af brottför hersins, og áformum íslensku þingmannanna sem sæti eiga á þingi þingmannsambands¢(cmp,stem-genpl|þingmannasambands) Nató um að að taka málið upp með öflugum hætti á vorþingi sambandsins sem nú stendur yfir í París, afréð Íslandsdeild sambandsins að senda öllum öðrum þingdeildum, sem aðild eiga að sambandinu, bréf með afstöðu Íslendinga og skýringum á atburðarrásinni¢(cmp,gensg-genpl|atburðarásinni).

Hér fer á eftir bréf íslensku þingmannanna. Bréfið er einsog$(cmp,adj+conj,1-2|eins og) það var skrifað – á ensku, svo enginn velkist í vafa um upprunalegan texta.

Í næsta pistli eru birtar upplýsingar úr merkilegustu svörunum, sem borist hafa, en þau styrkja að mati okkar þingmannanna stöðu Íslendinga verulega ef sækja þarf beint til Nató um lausn ákveðinna vandamála í tengslum við varnir landsins.

 Bréf Íslandsdeildar þingmannasambands Nató:

 Reykjavík, 6. apríl.

(Dear colleaque,

 Allow us to draw your attention to the rapidly developing situation regarding the security and defences of Iceland, a matter which was briefly touched upon at the Standing Committe meeting in Gdynia on 25 March.

 As you may be aware of, the US Government announced on 15 March the withdrawal of its fighter planes, helicopters and troops stationed in Iceland, no later than by September this year. For over 50 years, the US military has had a base in Keflavik, Iceland, in accordance with the bilateral Defence Agreement of 1951 between Iceland and the US. The Agreeement stipulates that the US, on NATO´s behalf and in accordance with its responsibilities under the North Atlantic Treaty, provides for the defence of Iceland, and that Iceland makes arrangements for the use of facilities needed for these defence purposes. The bilateral Defence Agreement with the US is a central pillar in Iceland´s security policy, since Iceland does not have a military of its own.

 Since the end of the Cold War, there have been systematic reductions in US military presence in Iceland, as has been evident in cases elsewhere in Western Europe.  Recently, negotiations took place between Iceland and the US on cost sharing with regards to the operation of Keflavik International Airport and the remaining defence equipment; the 4 fighter jets and the search and rescue helicopter squadron. The Icelandic government had proposed to bear greatly increased costs to this end when negotiations came abruptly to a halt as the US announced its unilateral decision to withdraw its defence equipment and military personnel. The Icelandic government very much regrets this decision.

 In this context it is worth noting that the Icelandic airspace is one of the largest in the world with almost 100 thousand aircraft passing through the Icelandic air traffic control area each year on transatlantic routes. Second, the capacity for helicopter search and rescue missions is greatly reduced which must be a concern for all states with sea vessels in the precarious North Atlantic. 

 The Defence Agreement between the US and Iceland is still intact and new negotiations have commenced on how the US intends to provide for the defence of Iceland, in accordance with its obligations under the Defence Agreement, after military withdrawal. Although we sincerely hope the negotiations will result in an acceptable solution for both parties, this situastion may call for a broader Nato involvement. Therefore, we want to make you aware of this new and developing security situation, as we obviously see this not as a localized Icelandic problem. Securing credible defences of Iceland and its airspace is of utmost importance to secure the vast North-Atlantic flank of NATO´s airspace.

Össur Skarphéðinsson, Head of delegation (sign.)
